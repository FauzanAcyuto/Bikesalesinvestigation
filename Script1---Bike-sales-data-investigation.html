The purpose of this project:

The bike store sales europe dataset contains the sales data of a bikestore with 18 columns and 113036 rows.

The purpose of this analyses is to figure out why the recorded profits in the data is not aligned with the order quantity, unit price, and unit cost data.

Bike Sales Dataset in Europe

Downloaded from <kaggle.com/code/sadiqshah/bike-store-sales-in-europe/data>

A simple calculation show how the recorded revenue is not aligned with the data. I took this chance to clean the data and calculate the gap of profit and the percentage of the revenue it represents

Cleaning and transforming the data

scleaned <- Sales %>% 
  clean_names() %>% 
  mutate(
    real_profit = (order_quantity * unit_price) - (order_quantity * unit_cost),
    profit_gap = real_profit - profit,
    p_gap_percent = profit_gap/revenue*100
  )

Solution:

I decided to do some data exploration with visualizations to uncover potential correlations in the data. This way we may be able to deduce the reason for this discrepancy.

The first potential correlation lies in my hypothesis, which is this discrepancy is caused by an unrecorded discount or tax scheme. So the first graph shows the relationship of revenue and profit gap

Create a graph showing the relationship of revenue and profit gap

scleaned %>% 
  ggplot() +
  geom_point(aes(x = revenue, y = profit_gap))+
  geom_smooth(aes(x = revenue, y = profit_gap))+
  labs(title = "The relationship between revenue and profit gap", 
       subtitle = 'shows clear indication of tax/discount levels',
       caption = 'data sourced from kaggle.com'
       )
## `geom_smooth()` using method = 'gam' and formula 'y ~ s(x, bs = "cs")'

These two graphs show that profit gap is not affected by date, rather it is affected by the revenue in each year

The next suspect for this profit gap is the product itself, there might be product specific discounts. In that case I calculated the average profit gap, max profit gap, and min profit gap to see if there is any pattern

Conclusion:

There seems to be a clear correlation between revenue/sales size and the profit gap. This makes sense if there is a discount or tax scheme that is structured based on each purchases size.

Limitation:

Although I have the data and analyses to strengthen my hypothesis. I can not say for certain that this conjecture is correct. Because there is a lack of qualitative data in this dataset to confirm or deny my hypothesis
